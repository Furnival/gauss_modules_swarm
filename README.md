# Gauss-Module Swarm Essays

This repository contains two short essays—**Every Angle at Once** and **Small Forces Patiently Applied**—that explore how Gauss modules might enable a coordinated swarm of Hall-thruster spacecraft.

Taken together, the essays consider the idea from two scales: one looks at geometry, perception, and distributed positioning around a target; the other looks at accumulation, where modest continuous thrust and careful coordination produce useful effects over time.

The project sits at the intersection of orbital mechanics, spacecraft architecture, and systems imagination. It is less a finished proposal than a disciplined thought experiment. I am hoping some NASA engineers might comment on the fesibility, but the essays are written for the general audience and I welcome comments.
